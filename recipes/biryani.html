Biryani

Description

You’ll find variations of Biryani all across the Indian subcontinent, from Pakistan to Bangladesh, Afghanistan to India. There are 2 main types – one where the protein and/or vegetables are cooked mixed throughout the rice, and the other version known as Hyderabad-style biryani in India where meat and rice are layered and cook in a sealed pot over fire. The latter is the style of biryani I’m sharing today.

There’s a wonderful Afghani restaurant in my area called Sahar (Newport, Sydney) which serves a Biryani that’s a huge favourite among locals. Hands down, the best ethnic restaurant I know in the upper northern beaches.

Ingredients

For Marinating Chicken:

  • Chicken – 500g (bone-in, cut into medium pieces)
  • Yogurt – ½ cup
  • Ginger-garlic paste – 2 tbsp
  • Red chili powder – 1½ tsp
  • Turmeric powder – ½ tsp
  • Garam masala – 1 tsp
  • Coriander powder – 1 tsp
  • Salt – to taste
  • Lemon juice – 1 tbsp
  • Fresh mint leaves – ½ cup (chopped)
  • Fresh coriander leaves – ½ cup (chopped)
  • Green chilies – 2 (slit)
  • Fried onions – ½ cup (store-bought or homemade)

For Rice:

  • Basmati rice – 2 cups (soaked for 30 minutes)
  • Water – for boiling
  • Salt – to taste
  • Whole spices:
    • Bay leaves – 2
    • Green cardamoms – 4
    • Cloves – 4
    • Cinnamon stick – 1 inch

For Layering:

  • Saffron – a few strands (soaked in 2 tbsp warm milk)
  • Ghee – 2 tbsp
  • Fried onions – ½ cup
  • Mint & coriander – a small handful each (chopped)

Instructions:

Step 1: Marinate the Chicken

  1. In a large bowl, mix all the marination ingredients.
  2. Add chicken and coat well.
  3. Cover and marinate for at least 1 hour (preferably overnight in the fridge).

Step 2: Cook the Rice

  1. Boil water in a large pot. Add whole spices and salt.
  2. Add soaked basmati rice.
  3. Cook until 70-80% done (rice should have a slight bite).
  4. Drain and set aside.

Step 3: Layer and Cook the Biryani

  1. In a heavy-bottomed pot or Dutch oven, add a layer of marinated chicken.
  2. Spread half the rice on top.
  3. Sprinkle some mint, coriander, fried onions, saffron milk, and a little ghee.
  4. Add the remaining rice and repeat the garnish.
  5. Cover tightly with a lid (or seal with dough to trap steam).

Step 4: Dum Cooking

  1. Option 1 – Stovetop: Place the biryani pot on a tawa (flat griddle) over low heat for 25–30 minutes.
  2. Option 2 – Oven: Bake at 180°C (350°F) for 25–30 minutes.

Let it rest for 10 minutes before opening. Gently fluff the biryani from the sides.

Serving Suggestions:

Serve with:

  • Raita (yogurt with onions/cucumber)
  • Boiled eggs (optional)
  • Salan (spicy curry) or pickle
